How to Use the Image to PDF Converter

To create a PDF from your images, start by opening the Images to PDF section and tap the Add Images button. You can select one or multiple images from your device at the same time. Once the images are added, they will appear in the workspace where you can preview them before creating your PDF.

If you have added several images, you can easily change their order before converting them. Use the arrow buttons on each image to move it left or right. You can also remove any image that you do not want to include in the final PDF. This makes it easy to arrange your pages exactly the way you want.

Before creating the PDF, you can edit your images using the available tools. You can crop an image, rotate it, adjust brightness and contrast, change saturation, apply filters, or add a custom text layer. If you want to place another image on top of your main image, the PIP tool can also be used for that.

After finishing your edits, tap the Next button to open the PDF preview page. Here, you can enter a name for your PDF and check the estimated file size. You can also adjust the compression quality depending on whether you want a smaller file or better image quality.

When everything looks good, tap the Download Compressed PDF button to create your PDF. The images will be combined in the same order as shown in the workspace. It is a good idea to check the preview and page order once before downloading the final file.

To convert a PDF into images, return to the main page and select the PDF to Images option. Tap the Select PDF File button and choose the PDF document from your device. The app will process the pages and display the converted images in the preview area.

Once the PDF pages have been converted, you can view the individual images directly in the app. If you need all the converted pages, use the Download All Images button to save them together. This is useful when you need to share or use individual PDF pages as image files.

The app is designed to keep the process simple, whether you are converting images into a PDF or extracting images from an existing PDF. You can use it for documents, scanned pages, notes, forms, certificates, or other images that you want to organize and convert quickly.

How to Use Excel to PDF and Excel to Image Converter

This app makes it easy to convert your Excel spreadsheets into PDF documents or image files without needing to install any extra software. Whether you have a small table, a report, a list, or a spreadsheet with multiple sheets, you can quickly turn your Excel data into a clean and easy-to-share format.

To get started with Excel to PDF, open the Excel to PDF option from the main dashboard and click the Add Excel button. You can select one or multiple Excel files from your device. The app supports common spreadsheet formats such as XLSX, XLS, and CSV, so you can work with different types of spreadsheet files in one place.

After selecting your Excel files, the app processes the spreadsheet data and prepares it for conversion. Each worksheet with available data is converted into a clean document-style layout. Empty sheets are skipped automatically, which helps keep the final result more organized and avoids unnecessary blank pages.

Once your Excel data has been processed, you can review the converted content in the workspace before creating the final PDF. This gives you a chance to check the pages and make sure the information you want to convert has been added correctly. If you are working with more than one Excel file, the sheets are added to the workspace so you can manage your complete project together.

When you are satisfied with the result, you can continue to the PDF creation step. Give your document a suitable name and choose the available quality or compression settings according to your needs. A higher quality setting can help maintain better image clarity, while a lower setting can be useful when you want a smaller file size for sharing or storage.

After everything is ready, use the download option to create and save your PDF file. The converted spreadsheet content is placed into the PDF in an organized way, making it convenient to share documents through email, messaging apps, cloud storage, or other platforms.

If you want to convert your Excel spreadsheet into images instead, choose the Excel to Images option from the main dashboard. Select your Excel file and wait while the app processes the available worksheets. The app reads the data from each sheet and converts the sheet into a clear image that you can preview before saving.

After the conversion is complete, the app shows the generated images in the preview area. You can check each image individually and remove any page that you do not want to keep. Each converted sheet can be saved separately, or you can use the Download All Images option when you want to save all the generated images together.

Excel to Image conversion can be useful when you want to quickly share spreadsheet information as a picture. Instead of sending an Excel file that someone needs to open in a spreadsheet application, you can simply share the converted image through messaging apps, social platforms, email, or other services.

The app also keeps the conversion process simple by automatically handling the spreadsheet data and creating a readable layout. It focuses on the actual content of your worksheets and avoids unnecessary empty rows or completely blank sheets, helping you get a cleaner result from your Excel files.

For the best results, it is a good idea to keep your Excel sheets properly organized before converting them. Make sure your important data is placed inside the worksheet and that the first row contains clear headings when possible. This helps the converted PDF or image look more structured and easier to read.

Whether you need to turn an Excel report into a professional PDF or quickly convert spreadsheet data into shareable images, this app provides both options in one convenient place. Select your Excel file, let the app process the sheets, check the preview, and download the format you need.
